MISSOURIAN MINUTE: How to make your Mother's Day flower bouquets last longer

BY SABRINA RUSSELLO  |   Sunday, May 10, 2015     6:00 a.m. CDT

COLUMBIA – Flowers are a favorite gift for Mother's Day, but they often wilt after just a few days. A local florist explains how to get the most out of your bouquet.

Tina Bradley, a floral designer at Allen’s Flowers, said it takes only a few tricks to keep flowers lasting longer.

Bradley said that cutting the stems every day or two will allow water to flow easily up the stems, keeping the flowers healthy.

"Flower stems are sort of like a straw, and if you use a scissor to cut your stems it smashes them," Bradley said. "Therefore, they don’t take up water as readily as they would if you had cut it with a knife." It is important to refill the vase with fresh water and flower food, she said.

Bradley said the flower food is key to making the pH balance, or acidity, of the water ideal. "The pH balance of your water is extremely important, so that the flowers can take it up a little easier," Bradley said. Bradley said a good pH balance is between a 4.0 and a 4.5.
